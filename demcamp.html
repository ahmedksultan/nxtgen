Democracy Camp '21

What is Democracy Camp?

We aim to provide comprehensive civic education to inquisitive middle school students, comprising a foundational knowledge of American government and politics. Our program will also explore what it means to be an American citizen, and in what forms meaningful participation in our political processes takes.

By definition, what we are doing is not revolutionary. The principal intention of our Democracy Camp is to cultivate prudent and informed students who care about politics—both its gravity, and its decency.

Democracy Camp consists of a three-week course designed to hone important skills for a new generation of student leaders. Our curriculum has been carefully developed to engage and challenge students while providing first-hand experience with the political landscape of today through activities, projects, research, and guest speakers. For more information, feel free to check out a detailed curriculum overview here.

When will Democracy Camp be held?

This year's program is scheduled to run between Monday, July 12th and Friday, July 30th. Each week will consist of three meetings, on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, from 11am to 1:30pm. Additional optional activities may be offered outside of this standard time frame.

Where will Democracy Camp be held?

Project NextGen's Democracy Camp is planned to be held virtually due to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, but as the situation develops, optional in-person activities may be offered.
